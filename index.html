The Field Guide · No. 01

The Giant-Killers

10 World Cup nights when the smallest team in the room refused to lose.

Every World Cup sells you the favourites. This is the other history — the nights the bracket broke, the holders went home, and a country nobody fancied walked off the pitch immortal.

1. USA 1–0 England · 1950

England arrived as the inventors of the game and a favourite to win it. A part-time American side — a dishwasher, a teacher, a hearse driver among them — wasn't supposed to lay a glove on them. Then Joe Gaetjens threw himself at a header before half-time. The English press reportedly thought the scoreline was a typo. It wasn't.

2. North Korea 1–0 Italy · 1966

Two-time world champions against a side almost no one in England had seen play. Pak Doo-ik struck just before half-time, the defence held, and Italy were out. Middlesbrough adopted the underdogs on the spot — still one of the most improbable results the tournament has produced.

3. Algeria 2–1 West Germany · 1982

The reigning European champions, humbled by a nation in its first ever World Cup. Madjer and Belloumi tore the favourites apart. What happened next — West Germany and Austria's mutually convenient 1–0 — is why every final group game now kicks off at the same time. The upset literally changed the rules.

4. Cameroon 1–0 Argentina · 1990

Defending champions. Maradona. Opening night. Cameroon finished it with nine men after two red cards — and still won, Omam-Biyik's header squirming under the keeper. The Indomitable Lions reached the quarter-finals and changed how the world saw African football forever.

5. Senegal 1–0 France · 2002

The holders. The world champions. Beaten in the very first game by a Senegal side on its tournament debut. Papa Bouba Diop's scrappy goal, and the shirt-by-the-corner-flag dance, became the image of the tournament.

6. USA 2–0 Portugal · 2002

A Portugal golden generation of Figo and Rui Costa, stunned 3–0 inside 36 minutes by a USA team no one rated. It announced the Americans as a side that could hurt anybody on the day — and it still defines a generation of US fans.

7. South Korea 2–0 Germany · 2018

The defending champions needed a win to survive. Two stoppage-time goals sent Germany out in the group stage for the first time in 80 years. Neuer caught upfield as Son rolled into an empty net — one of the great holders'-curse photographs.

8. Saudi Arabia 2–1 Argentina · 2022

Messi scored early. Then the 51st-ranked team in the world scored twice in five second-half minutes and defended like their lives depended on it — ending Argentina's 36-match unbeaten run. Argentina won the whole tournament. They lost only once all the way through: here.

9. Japan 2–1 Germany & 2–1 Spain · 2022

Not one shock but two. Japan came from behind to beat Germany, then did the exact same thing to Spain — identical 2–1 comebacks — to win the "group of death" ahead of both. Germany were eliminated in the group stage for the second World Cup running.

10. Morocco's run · 2022

Top of a group with Croatia and Belgium. Spain beaten on penalties. Portugal beaten 1–0. The Atlas Lions became the first African nation ever to reach a World Cup semi-final — carried by a wall of a defence and the loudest travelling support in Qatar.

"The favourites are never as safe as the bracket says. That's the whole reason we watch."
