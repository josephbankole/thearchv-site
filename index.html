      Football, explained

      Quick answers.

      The questions fans actually search, answered plainly.

      What does xG (expected goals) mean?
      xG rates the quality of a chance as a number between 0 and 1: the probability, based on historical data, that a shot from that position and situation ends in a goal. A penalty is worth about 0.76 xG.
      What is a false 9 in football?
      A false 9 is a centre-forward who drops deep into midfield instead of holding the front line. The movement drags defenders out of position and opens space for team-mates. Lionel Messi under Pep Guardiola is the classic example.
      How does the offside rule work?
      A player is offside if, at the moment the ball is played to them, they are nearer the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last defender, and they are then involved in the play. Standing in an offside position alone is not an offence.
      What is VAR in football?
      VAR is the Video Assistant Referee: an official who reviews video to help the on-field referee on four match-changing calls only: goals, penalty decisions, straight red cards, and mistaken identity.
